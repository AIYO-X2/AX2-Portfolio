Spatial Bodies in Motion: Rolled Flat
A Spatial Organization Of Boxes Birthed From The Motion Of The Human Body

Based on a roller-skating trick called "Shoot the Duck" where you squat down and balance on one foot while you extend your other out front with arms reaching, "Rolled Flat" is an analysis of the human body during this motion and a direct translation of it into an abstract composition of boxes. The focal point of the resulting box composition is the interaction and balance between tension and weight to create a stable center of gravity, wherein the body's tensions ends up at higher planes that support the weight regions situated lower down.

ELAC Environmental Design 101 / December 2020 / with Professors FAIA, D. Michael Hamner and Greg Kachoeff
